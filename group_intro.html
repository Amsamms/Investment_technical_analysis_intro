What is Technical Analysis?

Technical Analysis (TA) is a method of evaluating and forecasting future price movements of financial securities by analyzing historical market data — primarily price and trading volume — rather than examining a company's financial statements.

While Fundamental Analysis asks "What should this stock be worth?" by studying earnings, assets, and economic factors, Technical Analysis asks "Where is the price likely to go next?" by studying charts and patterns.

The 3 Core Principles

1. Market Discounts Everything All known information is already reflected in the current price.
2. Prices Move in Trends Prices tend to continue in a direction until something causes a reversal.
3. History Repeats Itself Patterns recur because human psychology (fear, greed) doesn't change.

How It Connects to Our Course

Our Investment course covers the Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH) in Chapters 9 and 12. EMH argues that if markets are truly efficient, past prices cannot predict future prices — which directly challenges Technical Analysis.

Our presentation bridges this gap: we present TA as the practitioner's counter-argument to EMH. Real traders use TA daily because markets are not perfectly efficient — behavioral biases, information delays, and herd behavior create exploitable patterns.

This makes our topic a natural extension of the course material — examining what happens when theory meets real-world trading.
